To the Woman I Hurt

This website is not here to erase what I did.

It is here because I want to be honest about the pain I caused you and take responsibility for it.

I Am Truly Sorry

I know that my actions hurt you deeply, and I want to say this clearly without excuses — I am truly sorry.

You did not deserve the pain that came from my actions, especially from someone who is supposed to care for you.

I Take Responsibility

I don't want to hide behind explanations or make my mistake sound smaller than it really is.

What I did was wrong, and I take full responsibility for it.

I know that apologies mean little without sincerity, and I want you to know that this apology comes from a place of deep regret.

I Understand the Hurt

Pain like this is not just about a single moment. It affects trust, peace of mind, and the way someone feels inside.

Knowing that I caused that pain in you is something I carry heavily in my heart.

What I Learned

Love is not only about affection or words.

It is about discipline, honesty, and protecting the heart of the person you care about.

I failed in that, and I deeply regret it.

My Intention

I am not asking you to ignore your feelings, and I am not asking you to rush forgiveness.

I only want to be honest about my regret and show you that I recognize the weight of what I did.

If there is ever a chance for me to rebuild what I broke, I know it will take patience, consistency, and real change from me.

— Mark

A Quiet Wish

Tonight, these lanterns rise into the sky carrying one quiet wish — that healing and peace find their way to your heart.

I am deeply sorry for the pain I caused you.

And if one day you allow it, I would sincerely work to rebuild the trust I broke.

Made with honesty.
